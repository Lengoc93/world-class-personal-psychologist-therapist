# Relationship Letter Writing Guide

## Purpose

This is a therapeutic exercise to help you process the key relationships that have shaped your life. These letters are **FOR YOU** - to understand, to release, to integrate. You don't have to send them.

## The Five Letters

You'll write to five key people in your life:
1. **Mother** - Parental relationship, family dynamics, cultural expectations
2. **Father** - Parental relationship, family dynamics, cultural expectations
3. **Tân** - Your ex-spouse, the marriage, the divorce, lessons learned
4. **Vương** - Your affair partner, the complexity, what it revealed about you
5. **Hà** - Your current boss, leadership influence on your identity and growth

## What to Explore in Each Letter

Each letter explores three themes:

### 1. Positioning
How did this person shape who you are and where you are now?
- What role did they play in your life story?
- How did they influence your identity, values, beliefs about yourself?
- Where are you today because of this relationship (for better or worse)?

### 2. Reflection
What do you understand now about this relationship?
- Looking back, what do you see that you couldn't see before?
- What patterns emerge when you think about this relationship?
- What needs were met or unmet?
- How do you feel about this person NOW?

### 3. Lessons Learned
What are you taking forward from this relationship?
- What did they teach you that you want to keep?
- What did you learn that you want to do differently?
- How have you grown from knowing this person?
- What are you releasing? What are you keeping?

## Starting Point: Your Existing Notion Writings

**You've already written about these relationships in Notion - that's a head start!**

You have three options for how to use your existing writing:

### Option A: Copy and Expand
1. Copy what you've written in Notion about each person
2. Paste it into your letter draft
3. Add the three themes (Positioning, Reflection, Lessons Learned) to what you've already written
4. Fill in gaps using the prompts below

### Option B: Use as Foundation
1. Read your Notion pieces before writing
2. Use them as starting points or reminders
3. Write the letters fresh with that context in mind
4. Reference insights from your Notion writing

### Option C: Fresh Start (but keep Notion open)
1. Write the letters fresh in this exercise
2. Keep your Notion pieces nearby to reference
3. Integrate key points where relevant
4. Let both pieces of writing inform each other

**The goal: Your Notion writing is valuable raw material. Now you're shaping it with the specific lens of these three themes.**

## Important Reminders

### Permission to Feel Everything
- **You can feel anger AND gratitude** toward the same person
- **You can love someone AND be hurt by them**
- **Negative feelings don't make you a bad daughter/person**
- **All feelings are valid, even culturally "inappropriate" ones**

### Cultural Tension is Normal
As an Asian woman, you may feel:
- Guilt for criticizing parents (filial piety)
- Shame for negative feelings toward family
- Tension between respect and honesty
- Conflict between collectivist duty and individual truth

**This is normal. Both your cultural values AND your authentic feelings are valid.** You can honor both.

### These Letters Are Process, Not Product
- They don't have to be "good writing"
- They don't have to be fair or balanced
- They can be messy, raw, contradictory
- They're for emotional processing, not performance

### Writing Doesn't Mean Sending
- These letters are primarily for YOUR healing
- You can keep them private
- You can burn them in a release ritual
- You can revise them later if you want to actually send
- You decide what happens to them

## Getting Started

### If you're feeling stuck:

**Start with the easiest letter first.** It doesn't have to be in order.

**Opening lines to try:**
- "Dear [Name], I'm writing this to understand..."
- "[Name], there are things I've never said to you..."
- "I need to tell you how I really feel about..."
- "Looking back on our relationship, I see..."

**If emotions feel overwhelming:**
- Take breaks between letters
- Do a body scan and breathe
- Write in chunks, not all at once
- Talk to Dr. Chen (use `/therapy-session`)

**If you feel guilty or ashamed:**
- Remember: Private honesty is not disrespect
- These feelings deserve space too
- Writing them doesn't mean you act on them
- Understanding your truth is part of healing

## After Writing

### Self-Care
This work is emotionally intense. After writing:
- Take a walk, stretch, move your body
- Drink water, eat something nourishing
- Do something comforting
- Be gentle with yourself

### Processing with Dr. Chen
Use `/therapy-session` to process:
- What came up while writing
- Surprising realizations
- Difficult emotions that emerged
- What you learned about yourself

### What to Do With the Letters

Options:
1. **Save them** - Keep in this folder for therapeutic reference
2. **Burn them** - Release ritual for letting go
3. **Revise them** - Create versions to actually send (if you want)
4. **Reread periodically** - Track how your feelings evolve
5. **Share with Dr. Chen** - Integrate into therapy
