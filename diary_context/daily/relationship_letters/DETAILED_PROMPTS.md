# Detailed Prompts by Relationship

Use these relationship-specific prompts when writing your letters. You can use all, some, or none - whatever helps you explore the three themes.

---

## For MOTHER

### Positioning (How she shaped you)
- How did she shape your beliefs about yourself, worth, and what you deserve?
- What cultural expectations did she pass down? Which do you keep, which release?
- How did she influence your view of marriage, relationships, success?
- What did you learn about emotions, love, duty from her?
- How has her influence shown up in your divorce and healing journey?

### Reflection (What you see now)
- What do you understand about her limitations now that you couldn't see before?
- How do you see the cultural context she was operating in?
- What generational patterns do you notice?
- How do your feelings toward her differ from earlier years?
- What do you wish she understood about you?

### Lessons Learned (What you carry forward)
- What values from her do you want to keep? Which do you release?
- How do you want to relate to family differently going forward?
- What cultural inheritance do you choose to keep? What do you let go?
- What did she give you that you're grateful for?
- What cycle ends with you? What continues?

## For TÂN (Ex-Spouse)

### Positioning (How he shaped you)
- What drew you to him? What did you hope this marriage would give you?
- How did the marriage shape your identity? Who were you as his wife?
- What did the relationship teach you about yourself - your needs, boundaries, patterns?
- How did the marriage ending change you? Who are you now that you weren't before?
- What role did he play in your growth (even through pain)?

### Reflection (What you see now)
- What warning signs do you see now that you missed then?
- What patterns from your family showed up in your marriage?
- What was your role in what went wrong? What was his?
- Do you have compassion, resentment, indifference, or a mix?
- What would you tell your past self about this relationship?

### Lessons Learned (What you carry forward)
- What did the marriage teach you about what you need in partnership?
- What red flags will you never ignore again?
- What did you learn about your own patterns, triggers, responses?
- What boundaries do you have now that you didn't have then?
- How has this shaped what you want (or don't want) in future relationships?

## How to Use These Prompts

### With Your Notion Writing

1. **Read your Notion pieces** about each person first
2. **Copy relevant parts** into your letter draft
3. **Use these prompts** to fill in gaps or go deeper on the three themes
4. **Weave it together** - your Notion writing + answers to these prompts = complete letter

### Without Notion Writing

1. **Pick 3-5 prompts** from each theme (Positioning, Reflection, Lessons)
2. **Answer them** as if talking to a close friend
3. **Shape into letter format** using the sample structure in LETTER_WRITING_GUIDE.md
4. **Add whatever else** wants to be said

### Mixed Approach

1. **Start with free-writing** - just pour out what comes
2. **Read these prompts** afterward
3. **Add answers** to questions you didn't naturally cover
4. **Polish** (or don't - raw is fine!)

---

## Remember

- You don't have to answer every question
- These are invitations, not requirements
- Let your Notion writing be the foundation
- Trust what wants to be said
- Be honest, even when it's hard

**Your truth is what matters here.** 🌸
