# Letter Writing Exercise - Acceptance Criteria (AC)

## Exercise Completion Criteria

This exercise is **COMPLETE** when you have met these criteria:

---

## ✅ **AC1: Five Letters Written**

**Criteria:**
- [ ] Letter to Mother - completed
- [ ] Letter to Father - completed
- [ ] Letter to Tân - completed
- [ ] Letter to Vương - completed
- [ ] Letter to Hà - completed

**Definition of "completed letter":**
- Minimum: At least 200 words (there's no maximum - write as much as you need)
- OR: You feel complete - you've said what needed to be said

**Quality over quantity:**
- A raw, honest 300-word letter is better than a polished 2000-word letter that avoids truth

---

## ✅ **AC2: Each Letter Addresses Three Core Themes**

For each of the five letters, you have explored:

### Theme 1: Positioning *(How they shaped who you are)*
- [ ] Identified this person's role in your life story
- [ ] Described how they influenced your identity, beliefs, or self-concept
- [ ] Explained where you are now because of this relationship (positive, negative, or both)

### Theme 2: Reflection *(What you understand now)*
- [ ] Reflected on what you see now that you couldn't see before
- [ ] Identified patterns in how this relationship unfolded
- [ ] Named what needs were met or unmet in this relationship
- [ ] Articulated how you feel about this person NOW (not what you "should" feel)

### Theme 3: Lessons Learned *(What you carry forward)*
- [ ] Named at least one lesson this relationship taught you
- [ ] Identified what you want to keep from this relationship
- [ ] Identified what you want to release or do differently
- [ ] Described how you've grown from knowing this person

**Note:** You don't need separate sections for each theme. They can be woven throughout your letter naturally.

---

## ✅ **AC3: Emotional Honesty**

**Criteria:**
- [ ] You wrote your REAL feelings, not what you "should" feel
- [ ] You included difficult emotions (if they exist) - anger, resentment, hurt, confusion
- [ ] You included positive emotions (if they exist) - gratitude, love, appreciation
- [ ] You didn't censor yourself due to cultural expectations, guilt, or shame
- [ ] You allowed contradictory feelings (loving someone AND being hurt by them)

**Self-check questions:**
- "Did I write what's TRUE for me, or what I think sounds 'good'?"
- "Did I avoid any difficult truths?"
- "Would I feel relief if this person truly understood what I wrote?"

**Pass = You were honest, even when it was hard**

---

## ✅ **AC4: Processing & Integration**

**Criteria:**

After writing all five letters, you have:

- [ ] **Read them back** to yourself
- [ ] **Noticed patterns** across relationships (e.g., recurring themes, similar dynamics)
- [ ] **Identified insights** about yourself (what you learned about your needs, values, patterns)
- [ ] **Discussed with Dr. Chen** (via `/therapy-session` or `/write-relationship-letter`) at least once
- [ ] **Decided what to do** with the letters (keep, burn, revise to send, etc.)

**Integration questions answered:**
- What themes appear across multiple letters?
- What do these five relationships teach you about yourself?
- How do these relationships connect to your current life chapter (divorce, identity, healing)?
- What are you ready to release?
- What do you want to carry forward?

---

## ✅ **AC5: Self-Care During Process**

**Criteria:**

Throughout the exercise, you:

- [ ] Took breaks when emotions became overwhelming
- [ ] Practiced at least one grounding technique (breathing, body scan, walking, etc.)
- [ ] Were gentle with yourself (no self-judgment for what you felt or wrote)
- [ ] Reached out for support if needed (Dr. Chen via `/therapy-session`)
- [ ] Acknowledged this is difficult work and gave yourself credit

**Self-care = You didn't force yourself through pain without care**

---

## 📊 **Success Metrics**

### Minimum Viable Completion (MVC)
To complete this exercise at a basic level:
- ✅ 5 letters written (at least 200 words each OR feels complete)
- ✅ Each addresses positioning, reflection, lessons learned
- ✅ Emotional honesty present
- ✅ One integration conversation with Dr. Chen

**Time estimate:** 3-6 hours total (across multiple sessions, not one sitting!)

### Full Completion
For maximum therapeutic benefit:
- ✅ All MVC criteria met
- ✅ Deep exploration of each relationship (500+ words per letter OR until you feel empty/complete)
- ✅ Pattern analysis across all five letters
- ✅ Integration conversation with Dr. Chen
- ✅ Decision made about what to do with letters
- ✅ Insights added to current therapy roadmap

**Time estimate:** 5-10 hours total

### Exceptional Completion
Going above and beyond:
- ✅ All Full Completion criteria met
- ✅ Second-round letters (revisiting after initial processing)
- ✅ Tracking how your feelings evolve over time
- ✅ If appropriate, revised versions prepared for actual sending
- ✅ Integration into long-term identity development work

**Time estimate:** Ongoing over weeks/months

---

## 🎯 **How to Know You're "Done"**

You've completed this exercise when you can honestly say:

1. **Completion**: "I've written to all five people and said what I needed to say."

2. **Honesty**: "I wrote the truth - my real feelings, not the 'nice' version."

3. **Exploration**: "I explored how each person shaped me, what I see now, and what I learned."

4. **Relief**: "I feel lighter, clearer, or more settled after writing (even if it was painful)."

5. **Integration**: "I understand patterns in my relationships and how they connect to who I am now."

6. **Forward movement**: "I know what I'm releasing and what I'm carrying forward."

## 🚀 **Getting Started (If You Haven't Yet)**

**Step 1:** Choose an approach
- Option A: Use `/write-relationship-letter` for guided prompts
- Option B: Free-write in blank files (see LETTER_WRITING_GUIDE.md)

**Step 2:** Start with the easiest letter first
- Doesn't have to be in order
- Build momentum with the one that feels most accessible

**Step 3:** Write one letter at a time
- Don't try to do all five in one day
- Space them out to process emotions

**Step 4:** Check off as you complete each AC
- Use the checklist above to track progress

**Step 5:** Integration session with Dr. Chen
- After all five letters: `/therapy-session`
- Discuss patterns, insights, next steps

---

## ⚠️ **What Completion Does NOT Mean**

Completion does NOT mean:
- ❌ You've resolved all feelings about these people
- ❌ You never think about them again
- ❌ You've forgiven everyone (forgiveness is optional, not required)
- ❌ You must send these letters
- ❌ Your relationships with these people are "fixed"
- ❌ You're "done" processing these relationships forever

Completion DOES mean:
- ✅ You've created a snapshot of where you are NOW
- ✅ You've given voice to your truth
- ✅ You've processed emotions that needed expression
- ✅ You've gained clarity and insights
- ✅ You've taken one significant step in your healing journey

---

## 🌸 **Final Note**

**This is not a test.** There's no "perfect" way to complete this exercise.

The acceptance criteria exist to:
- Give you clear direction (since you said that helps you!)
- Ensure you get therapeutic value from the work
- Help you know when you can feel genuinely complete
- Prevent you from stopping too early OR over-doing it

**Trust yourself.** If you meet the criteria but still feel incomplete, keep going. If you don't fully meet the criteria but feel genuinely complete, that's okay too.

**The goal is healing and understanding, not checking boxes.**
